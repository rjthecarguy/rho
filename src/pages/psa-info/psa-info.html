PSA Information

This test measures the activity in the prostate. Normal prostate levels should be between 0.00-4.00 ng/ml. A level above 4.0 ng/ml requires a urological evaluation. While under TRT this measurement may increase as testosterone begins to saturate the prostate. If your prostate increases too much it can lead to urinary changes or difficulties.

This test is often used as a marker for prostate cancer and is drawn at your initial consult. If your PSA level elevates more than 1.0 ng/ml in a year while under TRT further evaluation with a urologist may be warranted.
